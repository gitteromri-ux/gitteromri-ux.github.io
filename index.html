Hard caps

  • No rank may exceed 40% of segment calls unless its SOV/call lift over the next-best rank is ≥ 50%
  • No rep may exceed 15% of segment calls unless lift ≥ 50% AND confidence = High
  • Each rank receives ≥ 5% of calls AND each rep ≥ 30 contacted customers per 10-week window

Re-rank cadence

  • Re-rank weekly (Sunday) on the rolling 10-week window
  • Add a minimum-volume floor: rank = -1 if NumOfContacted < 20 OR NumOfCalls < 100
  • Add a SOV-weighted variant alongside reg-weighted ranks

Rank logic & method

How RankRegFromContacted and RankRegFromCalls are computed in the data warehouse.

vw_kpi_employee_rank — what it does

The view UNIONs five fact tables — fact_Leads (contacts), fact_Acquisitions, fact_Orders, fact_SAP (upsales), and fact_CallCenterCalls — at day × semester × language × employee grain, aggregates them to one weekly RankDate (each Sunday), and joins the rank that the employee held that Sunday from HelpDB.PowerBI.fact_EmployeesRank_NewLogic.

Two ranks are attached to every row:

  • RankRegFromContacted — quartile rank by registrations / contacted customers in the segment.
  • RankRegFromCalls — quartile rank by registrations / calls in the segment.

-1 means the rep had zero contacts/calls that week (treated as unranked). NULL means the rep was not in fact_EmployeesRank that Sunday.

How the quartile is assigned

  1. For each Sunday, look back 10 weeks per Employee × Category × Language.
  2. Compute RegFromContacted = Reg / Contacted and RegFromCalls = Reg / Calls.
  3. Within each (Date, Category, Language) group, sort by ratio. Run a volume-weighted cumulative quartile: position = (CumulativeVolume − ThisVolume/2) / TotalVolume. ≤25% → R1, ≤50% → R2, ≤75% → R3, >75% → R4.

What the rank rewards — and where it can mislead

Strengths

  • Simple, transparent quartile across (segment × language)
  • Volume-weighted cut prevents tiny-sample reps from dominating R4
  • 10-week look-back smooths short-term variance

Risks

  • Ignores SOV per order — a rep can be R4 on count but low on revenue
  • Self-reinforcing: R4 reps get more leads → numerator inflates
  • No minimum-volume floor — micro-volume reps can land in R4
  • New reps (IsBelow2Months) skewed low until window fills
